Create a better planet.

An open source energy system model initiative for Africa

OUR MISSION

Build an African open source energy system model prototype based on PyPSA until end of 2021

OUR VISION

Create an useful alternative to closed-source energy system model for industry and research. Improve the energy data landscape.

OUR VALUES

Freedom and openness is what we live and support. Collaboration is what we embrace. Ambition is what we dedicate our planet.

What we do

Create scalable open source model and database

Our model developments focus on scalable methodologies that can be applied in Africa and beyond. If regions want to contribute to a better database, they can easily do so and immediately benefit from it in our model.

Establish African open source network

As open source project we stand not only on the shoulder of giants, but also on the shoulder of the community. Building up a strong user, developer and maintainer network is essential for us.

Initiate a stable open source organization

Even open source projects need a stable organization to built trust and credibility. This is necessary to collect and allocate funds to brilliant contributers, making the tool most useful.

Empower Africa talents

We create opportunities for African talents to shape and improve their own energy future. Energy studies should not happen just about Africa, it should happen within Africa. The integration between the EU and AU model will lead to cross-fertilization beyond borders.

Checkout our Github repository

All of our model developments are open source and GPL3 licensed, meaning they must stay open. You can find our developments in the GitHub repository (click on header), where you can create issues, share ideas and discuss with us. Even the website you are looking at is open source. Feel free to use it and suggest improvements.

The documentation describes in more detail of how you can contribute, how our project is structured and provides the code documentation. Additionally, we share there learning materials and some relevant talks and papers in the roam of PyPSA and African modelling. The documentation is also open, feel free to make it better.
